Engagement Intention Dataset

Multi-modal dataset for engagement intention prediction in human-robot interaction

Vincent Pfaefflin
Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT)
Bosch Robotics Lab, Renningen

The dataset captures pre-engagement behavior in a multi-target environment with a stationary or moving robot, robot-centric 360° fisheye video and 3D full-body pose data.

Overview

This dataset is designed for studying whether a person intends to engage with a robot before the actual interaction begins.

Participants solve a puzzle task in an environment with six table stations and one robot station. As they move between targets, the data captures natural approach behavior, including cases where the robot is not the intended destination.

For detailed methodology, processing steps and evaluation settings, please refer to the paper.

Recording and labeling

Setup
  • Controlled indoor environment, roughly 7 × 7 m
  • 6 table stations and 1 robot station
  • Stationary and moving robot conditions
Modalities
  • 2 fisheye cameras with 360° robot-centric view
  • RGB video at around 15 Hz
  • 3D full-body pose with 30 joints at around 10 Hz
Labeling
  • Pickup and drop events define trajectories
  • Labels assigned from the trajectory destination
  • Robot-targeted trajectories form the positive class
